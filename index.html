About J.K. Rowling

[ Portrait of J.K. Rowling / Author Desk Image Placeholder ]

Joanne Rowling (born 31 July 1965), better known by her pen name J.K. Rowling, is a British author, philanthropist, and screenwriter. She is most famous for writing the Harry Potter fantasy series, which has won multiple awards, sold over 600 million copies, and become the best-selling book series in history.

The idea for Harry Potter came to her in 1990 while stuck on a delayed train from Manchester to London. Over the next seven years, she faced severe hardships—including the passing of her mother, divorce, and single parenthood—before completing Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone in 1997.

Beyond the wizarding realm, Rowling writes acclaimed adult crime fiction under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith. She is also deeply committed to philanthropic causes, supporting charities such as Lumos (which helps institutionalized children find families) and the Volant Charitable Trust.

Frequently Asked Questions & Trivia

Where do you get your character names? +

I collect unusual names from maps (like Snape and Dursley), saints (Hedwig), and war memorials (Lockhart). Some are completely invented, like Malfoy, Flitwick, and Quidditch!

What does the "K" stand for? +

Joanne does not have a middle name. Her publisher requested she use two initials to appeal to a broader audience of young boys, so she adopted "K" in honor of her paternal grandmother, Kathleen.

What was your favorite subject in school? +

English, of course! I actually wrote my very first continuous novel drafts tucked away during my English classes.

What advice do you give to aspiring writers? +

Read as much as you possibly can. It gives you an understanding of what makes good writing and what fails. Secondly, accept that you will write bad drafts before you write great ones—just keep pushing through!
